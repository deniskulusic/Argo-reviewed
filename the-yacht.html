FAQ

when is the ideal season for a croatian charter?

The premier cruising season in Croatia extends from late spring through autumn. While July and August offer the vibrant energy of the high season, June and September provide a more temperate and serene experience. For those seeking the ultimate in privacy and mild Mediterranean weather, May and October offer a peaceful, exclusive window to explore the Adriatic's hidden gems.

how is the charter rate for argo calculated?

The total investment for an Argo charter comprises the net charter fee plus the standard 13% Croatian VAT. Additionally, an Advance Provisioning Allowance (APA), typically 30% to 40% of the net fee, is allocated to cover individual expenses such as fuel, gourmet provisions, and tailored guest requests.

what is the guest capacity of argo?

Argo is uniquely designed to accommodate twenty-six guests in absolute comfort across thirteen residential suites. The configuration includes eleven sophisticated double cabins and two twin cabins, all featuring private en-suite facilities.

what is the customary gratuity for the crew?

While entirely at your discretion and based on your personal satisfaction, gratuities typically range between 5% and 15% of the net charter fee. This gesture serves as a distinguished acknowledgement of the crew's dedicated service and attention to detail.

where is argo's home port?

Argo is based in the historic city of Split. She occupies a premium position in the ACI Marina, offering guests a commanding view of the Diocletian's Palace and the charming Old Town.

is embarkation in dubrovnik possible?

Yes, we offer curated embarkation and delivery to Dubrovnik or any other destination along the Croatian coast. A delivery fee is applied, calculated purely on the fuel required to reach your preferred starting point.
